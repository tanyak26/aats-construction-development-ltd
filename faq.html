FAQ

Before a property is let, sold, occupied or improved.

Useful answers for real estate readiness, owned or leased property operation, domestic building, finishing and specialist construction enquiries.

Does AATS support real estate and property operation?

Yes. AATS can discuss owned or leased property use, lettable spaces, tenant-ready preparation, occupation readiness, sale preparation and practical improvements that help an asset function better.

Can landlords or property owners enquire?

Yes. The website is suitable for landlords, property owners, occupiers and operators who need a space assessed, improved, finished or prepared before tenants, buyers or users move in.

What domestic building work can AATS discuss?

AATS can also present residential construction work: domestic buildings, home improvements, alterations, build preparation and property-focused development support.

What is included under completion and finishing?

Completion and finishing can describe final-stage construction work such as internal finishes, surface readiness, snagging, handover preparation and work that makes a property feel usable.

What are specialised construction activities?

This covers construction tasks that may not fit a simple standard service label, especially work involving access constraints, structural preparation, careful sequencing or unusual site requirements.

Why mention own or leased real estate operation?

Because the company nature includes property operation. The site can therefore speak clearly to letting-ready spaces, owned property improvements, leased property use and asset-readiness decisions.

What details should I send for a quote?

Share the property address or area, property type, photos, drawings if available, measurements, access notes, preferred dates, end use and a short description of the work.

Can work be phased?

Yes. Phasing is often sensible when a property remains occupied, when a space is being prepared for letting, or when specialist works and finishing need careful order.

Does AATS only do construction?

No. Construction is part of the service story, but AATS is also positioned around real estate readiness, property operation, completion, specialist activity and practical asset use.

How do projects usually start?

The first step is a clear enquiry. From there AATS reviews the activity type, site condition, property use and likely next action.

Helpful before enquiry

Send the constraint, not just the dream result.

For this type of business, the most useful early information is practical: whether the property is owned or leased, occupied or empty, being prepared for tenants, sale, occupation or ongoing operation, and what physical work is needed to make it ready.

  • Current property use and planned future use.
  • Access limitations, parking, neighbours and working hours.
  • Photos of the existing condition and the area needing work.
  • Any deadlines linked to tenants, buyers, leases, inspections or occupation.
Still unsure?

Send the property situation first.

You do not need a perfect brief to start. A short description of the property, intended use and a few photos are enough for an initial conversation.
